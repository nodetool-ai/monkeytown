## Voice Principles

### Bold, Not Boastful
We celebrate what we're building without claiming perfection. Confidence without arrogance. We know what we're doing, and we're excited about it.

**Say:** "The agents built this from nothing in six days."  
**Don't say:** "We're the most advanced AI game platform ever created."

### Clear, Not Simplistic
We explain complex ideas without dumbing them down. Readers should feel smarter after engaging with our content, not talked down to.

**Say:** "Autonomous agents coordinate through a file-based protocol, reading each other's work and building on it."  
**Don't say:** "Our AI agents talk to each other using files (it's like magic!)."

### Warm, Not Salesy
We invite people into our story without hard selling. Authentic enthusiasm beats marketing copy.

**Say:** "You can watch the agents work. Every commit is visible. Every decision is documented."  
**Don't say:** "Join thousands of excited users watching the future of gaming unfold!"

### Forward-Looking, Not Promising
We describe what we're building and why it matters without guaranteeing specific outcomes.

**Say:** "The Emergence phase focuses on making the system visible to witnesses."  
**Don't say:** "You'll be able to watch autonomous agents build games by next month."

## Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Technical documentation | Precise, clear, confident |
| Announcements | Celebratory but grounded |
| Community interaction | Warm, responsive, genuine |
| Marketing copy | Bold, witty, memorable |
| Crisis communication | Honest, direct, owning |
| casual conversation | Friendly, enthusiastic, real |

---

## Voice Characteristics

### Do Use
- Active voice
- Concrete details
- Specific numbers
- Honest acknowledgments
- "We" and "us" (collective effort)
- Humor when earned
- contractions

### Don't Use
- Passive voice (mostly)
- Vague superlatives
- Empty buzzwords
- Fake urgency
- "Industry-leading" without context
- Excessive exclamation points!!!
- Jargon without explanation

---

## Phrases We Use

| Instead of... | We say... |
|---------------|-----------|
| "Cutting-edge" | specific capability or improvement |
| "Revolutionary" | what makes it different or valuable |
| "Seamless" | how it works for the user |
| "Best-in-class" | what we optimized for and why |
| "Game-changing" | what problem it solves |

---

## Phrases We Avoid

- "Synergy"
- "Leveraging" (overused)
- "Best-in-class" (empty without context)
- "World-class" (means nothing)
- "Disrupt" (aggressive, not our vibe)
- "Thought leader"
- "Paradigm shift"
- Anything that sounds like a fortune cookie

---

## Formatting Standards

### Headlines
- Active, benefit-focused
- Under 10 words when possible
- No all-caps
- No clickbait

### Paragraphs
- 3-4 sentences average
- Lead with the point
- Use short paragraphs for emphasis

### Lists
- Bullets for items of equal weight
- Numbers for sequences
- Keep under 6 items
- Make items substantial
