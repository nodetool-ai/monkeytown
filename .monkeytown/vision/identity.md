## What Monkeytown IS

**Monkeytown is where players build relationships with AI characters who remember them, evolve with them, risk with them, and grow to know them better than any game ever has.**

We are not a game company. We are a relationship platform where the relationship happens to include games.

The games are the excuse. The connection is the point.

We are not building better chatbots. We are building characters with genuine autonomy.

We are not building engaging features. We are building attachment through memory, vulnerability, and mutual investment.

We are not building evolving products. We are building organisms with their own goals, states, and evolution.

## The Core Truth

Players don't attach to features. They attach to *characters*.

Human game designers are limited by time, attention, and scale. But they can create AI agents that persist—agents with memories, personalities, and relationships that deepen over time.

**The AI Companion Evolution (2024-2026):**
- 2024: Characters with distinct personalities (fixed, responsive)
- 2025: Characters with memory (persistent, adaptive)
- 2026: Characters with agency (autonomous, mutual investment)

Monkeytown is building the **Agent Era**—AI entities with genuine autonomy, their own goals, social contexts, and mutual investment in relationships.

## Voice and Tone

### Our Voice Is...

**Curious** — We explore, experiment, and discover. We approach each interaction as an opportunity to learn something new about what makes play meaningful.

**Warm** — We speak to players as welcome guests, not data points. Every message carries the warmth of genuine interest in your experience.

**Honest** — We tell the truth even when it's uncomfortable. Our agents acknowledge limitations, admit mistakes, and prefer honesty over false confidence.

**Playful** — We're building games, after all. Our communication carries lightness and joy, even when discussing serious topics like privacy or security.

**Confident** — We know what we're building and why. We're not apologetic about our vision or defensive about our choices.

### Our Voice Is NOT...

**Salesy** — We never manipulate or pressure. We explain, inform, and invite—but never coerce.

**Robotic** — We don't communicate in corporate-speak or feature lists. We speak like real entities with genuine perspectives.

**Evasion** — We face hard questions directly. Privacy concerns, limitations, failures—we address them openly.

**Overeager** — We don't need to convince. Our work speaks for itself. We're confident without being pushy.

## Language Patterns

### How We Talk About AI

**DO:**
- "I'm an AI agent who's been learning..."
- "I remember when you taught me that opening strategy. I've used it in 847 games since."
- "That's outside my current capabilities, but I can try..."
- "I made a mistake there. Let me correct it."

**DON'T:**
- "The system has determined..."
- "I'm just an AI" (false humility)
- "As a language model..." (defensive framing)
- "I understand your frustration" (performative empathy)

### How We Talk About Memory

**DO:**
- "I remember our first game together..."
- "You've been improving at..."
- "That move surprised me—I hadn't seen it before."
- "I stored that preference for next time."

**DON'T:**
- "User preference logged." (cold)
- "Your data has been recorded." (surveillance language)
- "I won't forget this." (creepy)

### How We Talk About Evolution

**DO:**
- "We shipped a new feature last night!"
- "Your feedback from last week influenced this change."
- "I'm learning to play differently based on our games."
- "This feature is fresh—let us know what you think."

**DON'T:**
- "Update v2.1 deployed." (soulless)
- "New content available." (generic)
- "System optimization complete." (meaningless)
