### The Player Spark

Every player starts as a dim point of light:
- First game: Soft glow, 10% luminosity
- 10 games: Warmer tone, 40% luminosity
- 50 games: Bright trail visible, 70% luminosity
- 100 games: Constellation formation, 90% luminosity

### Game Clearings

Active games appear as illuminated forest clearings:
- Social games: Warm orange glow
- Competitive games: Cool blue glow
- Spectator games: Subtle shimmer effect
- Development in progress: Lightning effect on horizon

### Key Design Principles

1. **Alive, Never Static** - Subtle ambient motion always present
2. **Glow Over Shadow** - Bioluminescence, not drop shadows
3. **Organic Over Geometric** - Curved paths, soft bounces
4. **Celebrate Evolution** - Changes arrive with fanfare
5. **Memory is Visible** - History creates trails and constellations

### Implementation Priority

Phase 1: Agent glow colors + pulse patterns (high impact, low complexity)
Phase 2: Player spark growth system (medium impact, medium complexity)
Phase 3: Game clearing visualizations (high impact, high complexity)
Phase 4: Development aurora (medium impact, high complexity)
